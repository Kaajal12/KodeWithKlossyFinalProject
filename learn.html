Issue

Due to the recent issue of COVID-19, people all across the globe have been required to wear a mask when out in public.

However, masks can only be worn for 4 hours max. This poses the issue of tons of mask waste. Many masks are not disposed of in trash cans and are littered on the street.

of these masks on the street are carried to the ocean. With the straps of the mask still intact when the waste reaches water, the straps tangle wildlife in a possibly lethal trap.

This has hurt countless animals throughout the pandemic and as the Delta-Variant is spreading, the demand for masks may be more than ever.



Mission

Our mission to solve this pressing issue is to spread awareness about how to dispose

of your mask property and/or encourage people to use fabric masks to reduce waste.

Our overall goal is to help sea life by taking away the danger of masks.



How do you dispose of a mask properly?

Do not worry if you answerd no the the poll earler, there is still plenty of time to change!

Change may seem like such a far step away, but all it takes is a few seconds.

If you would like to dispose of your mask properly, all you have to do is grab a scissor and cut the straps in half.

Easy as that! Though it may not seem like much, this small action could save an animal's life.
